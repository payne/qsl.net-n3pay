VHF/UHF Radio

Very High Frequency (VHF) and Ultra High Frequency (UHF) bands for amateur radio.

  1. 2 meter band (144-148 MHz) - Most popular VHF band
  2. 70 centimeter band (420-450 MHz) - Primary UHF band
  3. Local repeaters and simplex frequencies
  4. FM voice communications and digital modes
  5. Emergency communications and ARES/RACES
  6. Handheld transceivers (HTs) and mobile radios
  7. Antenna considerations for VHF/UHF
  8. Line of sight propagation characteristics

HF Radio

High Frequency (HF) bands enable worldwide communications through ionospheric propagation.

  1. 80/75 meters (3.5-4.0 MHz) - Regional and DX communications
  2. 40 meters (7.0-7.3 MHz) - Reliable DX band day and night
  3. 20 meters (14.0-14.35 MHz) - Premier DX band
  4. 15 meters (21.0-21.45 MHz) - Excellent for DX during solar maximum
  5. 10 meters (28.0-29.7 MHz) - Skip propagation and local FM
  6. Ionospheric propagation and solar cycle effects
  7. HF antenna systems - dipoles, beams, and verticals
  8. Contest operation and DXpeditions

Digital Modes

Computer-based digital communication modes for amateur radio.

  1. FT8 - Popular weak signal mode for HF and VHF
  2. FT4 - Fast mode for contests and QSOs
  3. PSK31 - Phase shift keying for keyboard-to-keyboard
  4. RTTY - Radio teletype for contests and ragchews
  5. JT65 - Weak signal mode for VHF and microwave
  6. WSPR - Weak Signal Propagation Reporter
  7. VARA and Winlink for emergency communications
  8. Software setup - WSJT-X, fldigi, and sound card interfaces

  1. DMR - Digital Mobile Radio with talk groups

  4. P25 - Professional digital radio standard

  6. Hotspots and digital repeaters

  8. Internet linking and reflector systems

  2. HackRF One - Half-duplex transceiver SDR
  3. USRP devices - Universal Software Radio Peripheral
  4. FlexRadio systems - Commercial SDR transceivers
  5. GNU Radio - Open source SDR framework
  6. SDR Console, HDSDR, and CubicSDR software
  7. Signal processing and DSP concepts
  8. Remote operation and WebSDR systems
